This transcript appears to be from a motivational/productivity video about achieving goals in 2026. The speaker (likely named Dan) presents a comprehensive 6-step framework for goal setting and achievement.

## Key Concepts and Framework

### **Step 1: Set Power Goals**
- Focus on 12 specific goals for the year (one per month)
- Goals should be measurable and specific
- Avoid vague aspirations

### **Step 2: Time Audit (Red, Yellow, Green System)**
- **Red activities**: Energy-draining tasks that don't add value
- **Yellow activities**: Neutral tasks
- **Green activities**: Energy-giving, goal-aligned activities
- Action: Delete red tasks, delegate where possible, maximize green time
- Apply to both personal and professional time
- Goal: Make calendar full of "green" activities

### **Step 3: Create Daily Systems**
- **Systems = Save Yourself Time Energy Money Stress**
- **Morning routine**: Plan the next day the night before
- **Pomodoro technique**: 25-minute focused work sprints with 5-minute breaks
- **Music**: Use no-word EDM for focus
- **Review power goals 3 times daily**
- **Power of saying NO** to protect committed time

### **Step 4: [Implied - Building Habits]**
- Small consistent wins build big goals
- Systems beat motivation
- Use calendar to create repeatable systems

### **Step 5: Friend Inventory / Environment**
- Audit relationships (friends, family, colleagues)
- Set boundaries with people who hold you back
- Surround yourself with people who pull you forward
- "You're allowed to love family from a distance"
- Increase self-worth and set new standards

### **Step 6: Stay on Track (Measurement & Accountability)**
- **"What you measure gets managed"**
- **"Makes invisible growth visible"**

#### Three Implementation Methods:
1. **North Star Metric**: 
   - Pick ONE key metric for your goal
   - Track it obsessively every day
   - Example: body fat percentage, revenue, pages read, kilometers run
   
2. **Build a Scorecard**:
   - Track progress daily
   - Create tactical accountability
   - "The more you look at something, the higher probability of achieving it"
   
3. **Accountability Partner**:
   - Find someone you trust
   - Make public commitments
   - Create "positive peer pressure"
   - "You'll do 10x more for somebody else than for yourself"
   - Set stakes (example: Jen's story - lose 38 lbs in 4 months or lose job)
